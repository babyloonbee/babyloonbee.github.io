Homeschooled Kid Graduates High School 5 Months Before Birth
January 17th, 2019
30.1kShares
26.6k Share
3.6k Share
Share

GASTONIA, NC—A local homeschooled child has graduated a full 5 months before being born, sources confirmed.

The Miele family was proud to announce that the youngster has achieved her high school diploma at least six years earlier than expected, all because of their advanced homeschooling techniques.

Little Hannah Miele's mother played their custom curriculum for her via a pair of womb headphones. The child developed an innovative series of kicks and punches similar to Morse code to give her answers to quizzes and tests and ask questions. Her parents said she had a particular interest in ancient epic poetry, though she grew fussy if they ever played the Iliad to her in anything other than the original Greek.

"She advanced much more quickly than we expected," Miele's mother said, beaming. "We were hoping she'd at least get through middle school by the third trimester, of course, but we didn't want to put any high-pressure expectations on her."

At the baby's college reveal party, her parents broke open a balloon, which revealed she was going to attend Liberty University, a private Christian college. "We're so happy," said Mr. Miele. "A publicly educated unborn baby probably would have picked a state school."

At publishing time, the family's 4-year-old was scolded for not having been accepted to any colleges yet.
